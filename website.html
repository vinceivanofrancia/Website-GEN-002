The Issue: A Flood of Corruption?

An analysis of the 2024 Flood Control Scandal

The Budget vs. The Reality

Under the current administration, the Department of Public Works and Highways (DPWH) was allocated a staggering ₱545 billion for flood control projects. Despite this massive funding—larger than the budget for agriculture or defense—Metro Manila and nearby provinces were submerged during Typhoon Carina in 2024.

  • 421+ "Ghost Projects": Senate hearings revealed hundreds of projects that were paid for but either never existed or were abandoned.
  • Zero Allocations in Critical Areas: Investigations found that flood-prone towns in Bulacan received zero flood control infrastructure, while funds were diverted to non-critical districts favored by politicians.

Allegations of Systemic Corruption

The core of the scandal involves allegations of a "kickback" system embedded in the infrastructure sector.

  • The 20-40% Kickback Scheme: Witnesses at Senate hearings testified that up to 40% of a project's total cost is siphoned off as bribes to legislators and district engineers.
  • Substandard Materials: To recover the cost of bribes, contractors allegedly use cheaper, substandard cement and steel, resulting in dikes and spillways that crumble under pressure.
  • Monopoly of Contracts: A specific group of contractors (e.g., relating to the Discayas and Cos) reportedly cornered billions in contracts.

The Human Cost

The failure is not just financial; it is humanitarian. The lack of effective flood control has led to:

Displacement

Thousands of families in Metro Manila and Bulacan lose their homes annually due to rising waters.

Economic Loss

Billions in agricultural crops and business revenue are wiped out, keeping the poorest sectors in poverty.

Recommendations & Solutions

Moving from corruption to construction: How we fix the crisis.

Digital Accountability

Implement Blockchain-based procurement. By digitizing the bidding and payment process, every peso can be tracked in real-time, making "ghost projects" impossible to hide from public audit.

Nature-Based Solutions

Shift focus from purely concrete structures to "Sponge City" concepts. Restore wetlands, mangroves, and permeable surfaces that naturally absorb water.

Citizen Oversight

Institutionalize Participatory Auditing. Allow NGOs and civil society groups to inspect projects during construction ensuring specifications are met before payments are released.
